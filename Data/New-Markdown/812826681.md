## 6. Organization Core Information
### 6.1. Basic Details
- **organizationName:** The Wildflower Foundation
- **employer_id:** 812826681
- **founded_year:** 2016
- **location:** 1330 Lagoon Ave Suite 4thFloor
- **zip:** 55408
- **state:** MN
- **city:** Minneapolis

### 6.2. Mission
- **mission:** Provide authentic Montessori education through small, teacher-led, community-embedded schools that support diverse students and educators across the United States and Puerto Rico.

### 6.3. Key Activities
- **Activity 1:** - Provide startup grants and low-interest loans to Montessori teacher-leaders
- **Activity 2:** - Offer coaching and administrative support for Montessori microschool operations
- **Activity 3:** - Facilitate a peer support network for Montessori teacher-leaders
- **Activity 4:** - Expand access to authentic Montessori education for low-income students
- **Activity 5:** - Increase racial and ethnic diversity among Montessori students and educators
- **Activity 6:** - Support public funding access for community-embedded Montessori microschools
- **Activity 7:** - Scale the Wildflower school model across multiple states and territories

### 6.4. Potential Risks
- **Risk 1:** Financial risk: Dependency on startup grants and low-interest loans for Teacher Leaders, which may create funding uncertainty
- **Risk 2:** Financial risk: 41% of students come from low-income households, potentially impacting tuition revenue
- **Risk 3:** Operational risk: Reliance on Teacher Leaders to run schools, which could lead to challenges in scaling and consistency
- **Risk 4:** Strategic risk: Rapid expansion to over 70 schools across multiple states may strain resources and quality control
- **Risk 5:** Compliance risk: Operating in 18 states plus Washington D.C. and Puerto Rico exposes the organization to varied regulatory environments
- **Risk 6:** Reputational risk: Focus on serving diverse communities and low-income students may face challenges in maintaining consistent quality across all schools

## 8. Outcome Metrics
### 8.1. 2024
- **Metric 1:** Of approximately 1300 students enrolled in Wildflower schools, 64% identify as Black, Indigenous, and/or People of Color.
- **Metric 2:** 43% of children and families live in families that qualify as low-income according to the federal Department of Housing and Urban Development.
- **Metric 3:** 26% of students speak a language other than English as their first language.
- **Metric 4:** Wildflower’s Family Net Promoter Score is 84, reflecting nearly universal enthusiasm among families for their children’s teachers and school communities.
- **Metric 5:** Currently, 90% of Teacher Leaders remain in their schools each year.
