### 6.2. Mission
- **mission:** To provide equitable access for Latino children and families to resources, support, and opportunities needed to thrive and achieve social and economic justice.

### 6.3. Key Activities
- **Activity 1:** - Deliver two-generation parent-child education programs to Latino families
- **Activity 2:** - Provide high-quality early childhood education and Head Start services
- **Activity 3:** - Develop community leadership skills among Latino parents and caregivers
- **Activity 4:** - Support and improve quality of home-based child care providers
- **Activity 5:** - Offer college preparation and workforce development for Latino families
- **Activity 6:** - Advocate for educational equity and social justice for Latino communities
- **Activity 7:** - Cultivate partnerships to expand resources and opportunities for Latino families

### 6.4. Potential Risks
- **Risk 1:** Financial risk: Dependency on grant funding and donations for operations, as evidenced by multiple references to funders and donors
- **Risk 2:** Operational risk: Potential resource constraints in serving a large geographic area across multiple states and programs

- **Risk 4:** Compliance risk: Complexity of managing regulatory requirements across multiple Head Start and Early Head Start programs in different regions
- **Risk 5:** Reputational risk: High-profile board members and partnerships create elevated expectations for organizational performance and impact

## 8. Outcome Metrics
### 8.1. 2023
- **Metric 1:** 72% of parents in our Parent-Child Education Program (PCEP) increased their knowledge in early childhood development.
- **Metric 2:** 64% of parents in AVANCE College enrolled in continuing education.
- **Metric 3:** 58% of parents in PCEP demonstrated an increase in parent connectedness.
- **Metric 4:** Children in our Head Start and Early Head Start programs had an 86% rate of meeting or exceeding standards in school readiness.
- **Metric 5:** Children who participated in PCEP were more likely to attend pre-K and have better attendance compared to a demographically similar group.

### 8.2. 2024
- **Metric 1:** A total of 1,576 children and 1,391 caregivers were served through the Parent-Child Education Program (PCEP) in the year.
- **Metric 2:** The Head Start and Early Head Start programs achieved a 91% enrollment rate and 88% of children met or exceeded school readiness standards.
- **Metric 3:** The Families Building Communities (FBC) program expanded to new sites, increasing its graduates from five to 47 this year.
- **Metric 4:** The AVANCE to College program served 47 participants, marking a 61% increase from the previous year.
- **Metric 5:** Our Quality Child Care Matters (QCCM) program supported 78 family child care providers in enhancing their operations and quality of care.
