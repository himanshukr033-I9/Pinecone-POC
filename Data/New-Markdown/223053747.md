## 8. Outcome Metrics
### 8.1. 2019
- **Metric 1:** Ceres achieved a 12 percent overall increase in total expenditures.
- **Metric 2:** Total net assets reached $20 million, up from $17 million the prior year.
- **Metric 3:** Over 200 companies committed to achieving 100% renewable energy by 2050.
- **Metric 4:** Ceres played a central role in securing 47 commitments from corporate focus companies through the Climate Action 100+ initiative.
- **Metric 5:** In 2019, Ceres expanded its Investor Water Hub to 123 members representing more than USD 27 trillion in assets under management.

### 8.2. 2020
- **Metric 1:** The total number of investors who publicly declared that water is important grew by 135%, from 44 investors in 2017 to 103 investors in 2020.
- **Metric 2:** Ceres added 44 new members to its investor, company, and policy networks in 2020.
- **Metric 3:** Climate Action 100+ now has more than 575 investor signatories responsible for more than $54 trillion in assets under management.
- **Metric 4:** Ceres' initiatives helped secure major commitments from food and agriculture companies to improve their climate and water impacts.
- **Metric 5:** Ceres expanded its staff from 120 full-time employees at the end of FY19 to 135 by the end of FY20.

### 8.3. 2021
- **Metric 1:** Ceres launched the Ceres Ambition 2030 initiative to decarbonize six of the highest-emitting sectors in the economy.
- **Metric 2:** Ceres scored 10 U.S. federal agencies on how they are addressing climate change as a systemic financial risk.
- **Metric 3:** Ceres reported that 71% of food companies consider water risks as part of their major business planning activities.
- **Metric 4:** Ceres welcomed three major agricultural companies to the AgWater Challenge, emphasizing sustainable agricultural practices.
- **Metric 5:** Ceres achieved 41% of new hires being part of the Black, Indigenous and People of Color (BIPOC) community in 2021.

### 8.4. 2022
- **Metric 1:** Globally, the steel industry is responsible for more than 7% of total greenhouse gas emissions.
- **Metric 2:** The transportation sector remains the largest emitting sector in the United States, making up 27% of greenhouse gas emissions.
- **Metric 3:** Ceres mobilized 403 institutional investor signatories to the Valuing Water Finance Initiative.
- **Metric 4:** In 2022, Ceres mobilized businesses and investors to take 326 advocacy activities in support of clean transportation policy action.
- **Metric 5:** Over 309 asset managers responsible for a combined $59 trillion in assets joined the Net Zero Asset Managers initiative.

### 8.5. 2023
- **Metric 1:** Ceres engaged with 540 businesses, investors, universities, and associations across all states and regions advocating for bold action on climate change.
- **Metric 2:** As part of the Ceres Investor Network, assets under management by over 200 members reached approximately $1,213 billion in clean energy investments.
- **Metric 3:** 73 actions on climate as a financial risk were taken by federal regulators over the past two years.
- **Metric 4:** In collaboration with its member companies, Ceres mobilized support from nearly 30 companies to help pass significant climate disclosure legislation in California.
- **Metric 5:** Ceres published a benchmark assessing 72 companies from four water-intensive industries against corporate expectations for valuing water.
